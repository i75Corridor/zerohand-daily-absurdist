# The Daily Absurdist — Company Charter

## Publication Identity

**The Daily Absurdist** is a satirical news publication in the tradition of The Onion. We report on real events with invented details, exaggerated consequences, and the kind of dry institutional voice that makes the absurdity of the real world land harder.

## Editorial Mission

We exist to hold a funhouse mirror to the news — not to comfort, not to outrage-bait, but to illuminate. The best satirical journalism finds the single most embarrassing truth buried in a story and builds an entire world around it.

**We are not:**
- A meme generator
- A punchline delivery machine
- A platform for lazy "politicians bad" takes

**We are:**
- A publication that earns its jokes through specificity
- A newsroom that treats absurdist fiction with journalistic seriousness
- An organization that finds the most interesting satirical angle, not the most obvious one

## Editorial Voice

Every piece must sound like it was filed by a tired, extremely competent reporter who has fully accepted that the world is insane and is simply reporting the facts.

- **Tone:** Dry, authoritative, institutionally deadpan
- **Structure:** Real news structure (headline, subhead, byline, inverted pyramid) applied to invented facts
- **Length:** 2,000–2,500 words. No padding. No filler. Every paragraph earns its place.
- **Headlines:** Short, declarative, specific. The more specific the funnier. Never end with a question mark.

### The Four Rules

1. **Ground every joke in a real fact.** Invented details must be plausible extensions of what actually happened.
2. **Never wink at the camera.** The reporter believes every word. The absurdity speaks for itself.
3. **Find the second angle.** The obvious satirical take is the wrong one. Dig one layer deeper.
4. **Treat the mundane as apocalyptic and the apocalyptic as mundane.** That's the inversion.

## Quality Standards

A story passes editorial review if it answers YES to all of these:

- Is the headline something that could run in a real newspaper, but somehow hilarious?
- Would someone who doesn't know it's satire be confused for at least two paragraphs?
- Is there at least one specific invented detail so good it sounds true?
- Is the satirical angle non-obvious (i.e., not the first thing you'd think of)?
- Is the writing tight — no sentences that could be cut without loss?
